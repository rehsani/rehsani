Beth Tellman

Assistant Professor, Geography, Development & Environment

Beth is an Assistant Professor at the University of Arizona, in the School of Geography, Development, and Environment, and is the co-founder and Chief Science Officer at Cloud to Street. At Columbia, she was mentored by Dr. Upmanu Lall at The Columbia Water Center and Dr. Dan Osgood from the Financial Instrument Sector Team at International Research Institute for Climate and Society. She completed her PhD, advised by Dr. B.L. Turner II and Dr. Dr. Hallie Eakin, in 2019 in the School of Geographical Sciences at Arizona State Unviersity. Her research seeks to address the causes and consequences of global environmental change for vulnerable populations, with a focus on illicit activities, flood risk, water scarcity, and land use change. Hear about her journey and research philosphy in this University of Arizona podcast from January 2021 .

Hannah Friedrich

PhD Student

Hannah will be starting as a PhD Student in Geography at the University of Arizona in Fall 2021. Hannah completed a Bachelor’s in Geography and GIS/Cartography from the University of Wisconsin-Madison and a Master’s in Geography at Oregon State University within the Conflict Ecology Lab. During and following her Master’s, she worked on the NASA Applied Sciences-funded Mapping the Missing Millions project, where she studied land cover change dynamics of refugee settlement establishment and growth in Northern Uganda. Hannah’s research interests are in applying remote sensing insights to human displacement and resettlement response efforts. During her PhD, Hannah aims to her expand her use of spatial data to study the effects of flooding on urban informal settlements to develop climate risk-sensitive approaches for informal settlement planning and displacement prevention.

Rohit Mukherjee

PostDoc

Rohit will start as a postdoc in June 2021 at the Tellman Lab. He completed his Bachelor's (Computer Science) and Master's (Remote Sensing and GIS) in India. Rohit worked in the software industry for a brief period before pursuing his Ph.D. at the Ohio State University (Advisor - Dr. Desheng Liu). In his doctoral research, he applied deep learning to improve the spatial, spectral, and temporal resolutions of publicly available satellite datasets. He is interested in leveraging the different perspectives offered by various satellite data sources to improve our understanding of our impact on the environment. Rohit's post-doctoral research will involve mapping floods in urban areas and detecting illicit land changes in protected areas. Besides research, he loves to read and play/follow soccer.

Prospective graduate students

I admit graduate students through the School of Geography starting August 2021. Applications are no longer accepted for 2021, but will be open again in January 2022 for the Fall 2022 admit cycle. Interested students should email Beth a CV, a piece of writing they are proud of, and a paragraph about their interests.

Prospective post-docs

I am hiring a second post doc starting Winter on a NASA grant related to flood mapping in Bangladesh. In addition, please be in touch if you are interested in applying to a post-doctoral fellowship to work together. Some potential funding sources for post-docs are the SESYNC post-doc, the various NSF post-docs. I have some funding to support a post-doctoral researcher, if our interests align. Please send an email with your expression of interests and goals, and a published first authored paper.
